See Glass?

Brown, clear, transparent. Ah, peons.
Ample green, splash of amber. Signs of the middle class.
Amethyst, touch of pink, aqua, cobalt. Adorn the upper few.
Turquoise, red, yellow, black, teal. Enter the elite.
But orange? The rarest of the rare.
Nothing rhymes with orange.

Orange thats been through the wringer.
Orange thats old and worn.
Lost its sheen; opaque.
No rough edges. No shards.
Orange thats forgotten it’s ancestry.
That orange? That orange is Queen.

And Amberina is the Queen amongst queens.
By definition a centurion.
Rarely washed up; rarely seen.
If sighted, count your blessings,
And she may return the favor.
Yes, definitely pick her up.
Oh, the things she’s seen.
She’s tired and she’s earned it.

And yet, from brown to orange.
From the peons to the Queen.
They’re all garbage. Waste. Refuse.
Broken, thrown, ignored. Cast adrift.
The odd one, perhaps, with a message,
Striving to reach another shore.
And yet, still trash.

But what trash, what treasure.
If the tides and wind and sand have had their say.
And scratched their way in,
Ground their way around,
Pummeled and tumbled and stirred.
Mother Nature has had her say.
And added her unique value.

The Queens, the elite, even the upper middle,
Grace necks, ears, wrists and side tables.
Adorn lights and windows and mirrors. Proudly displayed as individuals.
And, as always, the lower class and below,
Gather together, in clunky vases and jars,
Some still sharp; many transparent to a fault.
A cacophony of reflected light.
Showing the beauty of community.

If any catch your eye,
Stop, bend, gather, treasure.
For while you search for Amberina,
Remember that you are, simply, collecting trash.

Beach / Sea glass follows a scale, based on how worn / old it is.

Beach glass is 'found at the beach', which is a pretty low bar; it may be very fresh (sharp edges, filled with beer last night) or slightly worn (dull edges, but still transparent).

Sea glass is opaque; it has spent enough time in mother nature's washing machine that it is worn smooth. Sea glass, obviously, is more valuable than just beach glass.

You are more likely to find sea glass in an 'old' location where glass was often thrown away, dumped off ships years and years ago. Lahaina, being the center of the whaling industry, has that attribute. We were on Lahaina's Baby Beach after the 2022 storm 'Darby' and literally picked up a pound of glass - competing to get pieces with others doing the same. Most of it was beach glass, but there was some good sea glass as well. We have been back many times since - at low tide, after larger waves - and glass has been few and far between.

Sea glass is becoming harder to find. Less containers are glass nowadays, and rules against littering are stronger.

Elusive

A pause reflective steals away the light
A single glimpse of the beauty within
Overturns the drive to stride with haste alight
Lest that glance dissolve to memory again

Silent now standing senses attuned
Watching intently for a glint, a flash
Amoung the hoards of uninspired marooned
Upon the shores unexpected did crash

Not waiting quietly for the lazy hand
Rather fighting the currents and the surf
Mixing tumbling hidden then touching land
Staying camouflaged amoungst the scurf

This elusive active dance of wave
Makes this sea glass a higher form of crave
Take salty water
Add a vigorous sand stir
Watch sea glass emerge

Once a vessel
Now thouroughly mixed up
Found a way to shore

Once sand heated high
From glass to shard to seaside
Now returns to sand
There was a lass from Hawaii
Who threw a bottle with glee
In pieces it embraced the ocean
Spent ages rocking with motion
Then emerged most opaquely

Hi ho and a bottle of rum
Made the sailor completely numb
Overboard she went
Her contents spent
A double example of dumb

There was a lad most crass
Who reveled in breaking bottles with his ass
He'd give the girls what they please
By giving his cheeks a good squeeze
Thank god the sea washed that glass
From sand
Fierce fire forged glass
Human habits had their harried way
Brazenly broken bottles brave the sea
Water washes well the wonton waste
Salty sand scratches surfaces so
Glass grinds and grates until
Returning recycled and renewed
To sand
